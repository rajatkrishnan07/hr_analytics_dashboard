## Problem Statement

This HR Analytics Dashboard aims to provide insights into various HR metrics to help the HR department of a company make informed decisions. By analyzing employee data, the dashboard assists in understanding employee satisfaction, performance, and retention. It allows HR managers to identify areas of improvement, monitor key performance indicators, and make strategic decisions to optimize workforce management.

    Step 1: Handle Missing Values

•   Identify columns with missing values using summary statistics or visualization techniques.

•	Decide on the appropriate strategy for handling missing values (e.g., imputation, removal).

•	Use Power Query Editor in Power BI Desktop to filter out or replace missing values based on the chosen strategy.


    Step 2: Remove Duplicates
![duplicatevals](https://github.com/rajatkrishnan07/hr_analytics_dashboard/assets/101494311/33b37414-69d5-4f74-adc5-d8125be8df6e)

•	Remove duplicate records using Power Query Editor by deduplicating based on selected columns.

•	Verify that duplicate records have been successfully removed to ensure data integrity.


    Step 3: Standardize Formats

 •	Identify columns with inconsistent formats (e.g., date formats, categorical variables).

•	Standardize date formats using date parsing functions or transformations.

•	Standardize categorical variables by ensuring consistent naming conventions or encoding categorical values.

•	Use Power Query Editor to apply format standardization transformations across relevant columns.

    Step 4: Data Validation

•	Validate data consistency by checking for anomalies or unexpected values.

•	Verify numerical ranges, date ranges, and other constraints to ensure data accuracy.

•	Correct any data anomalies or errors found during validation using appropriate data cleaning techniques.


    Step 5: Address Outliers

•	Decide on the appropriate treatment for outliers (e.g., removal, transformation, winsorization).

•	Verify the effectiveness of outlier treatment and ensure that data distribution is within acceptable bounds.

Insights on Attrition   
    
    1. Attrition Rate Analysis
•	Calculate and visualize the attrition rate over time to understand trends in employee turnover.
•	Identify periods of high attrition and investigate potential reasons behind them.

    2. Attrition by Department
•	Analyze attrition rates across different departments or teams to pinpoint areas with the highest turnover.

•	Identify departments that may require targeted retention strategies or improvements in work environment.

    3. Attrition by Employee Characteristics
•	Explore attrition rates based on various employee characteristics:

o	Education: Analyze attrition rates by education level to understand how educational background influences turnover.

o	Age Group: Investigate attrition rates across different age groups to identify age-related trends in employee turnover.

o	Salary Slab: Assess attrition rates by salary slab to determine the impact of compensation on employee retention.

o	Job Satisfaction: Examine attrition rates based on employee satisfaction levels to gauge the correlation between job satisfaction and turnover.

o	Monthly Income: Analyze attrition rates by monthly income to understand how income levels affect employee retention.

o	Years at Company: Investigate attrition rates based on the number of years employees have been with the company to identify turnover patterns over time.

o	Job Role: Assess attrition rates by job role or position to understand turnover dynamics across different roles within the organization.

o	Gender: Explore attrition rates by gender to identify gender-specific trends in employee turnover.

    4. Attrition Predictive Factors
•	Investigate potential factors contributing to employee attrition, such as job satisfaction, performance ratings, and salary levels.

•	Use predictive analytics techniques to identify employees at risk of leaving and take proactive measures to retain them.

    5. Attrition Cost Analysis
•	Estimate the financial impact of attrition by calculating the cost of employee turnover, including recruitment, training, and lost productivity.

•	Highlight the importance of reducing attrition for cost-saving purposes and overall organizational stability.

    6. Attrition Intervention Effectiveness
•	Evaluate the effectiveness of retention strategies and interventions implemented to reduce attrition.

•	Monitor changes in attrition rates after implementing initiatives such as mentorship programs, career development opportunities, or performance incentives.

    7. Attrition Benchmarking
•	Compare your organization's attrition rates with industry benchmarks to assess competitiveness and identify areas for improvement.

•	Benchmarking can provide insights into whether attrition rates are within acceptable ranges and help set realistic retention targets.

    8. Attrition Impact on Organizational Culture
•	Assess the impact of attrition on organizational culture and employee morale.

•	Understand how frequent turnover affects team dynamics, productivity, and overall workplace satisfaction.

    9. Attrition Mitigation Strategies
•	Develop and implement targeted strategies to mitigate attrition, such as improving employee engagement, offering competitive benefits, and providing opportunities for career advancement.

•	Continuous monitoring and adjustment of retention strategies based on data-driven insights are essential for effectively reducing attrition rates.
